# Common Knowledge - General Evaluation Dataset
# Everyday facts, general knowledge, and widely-known information

Water freezes at 0 degrees Celsius (32 degrees Fahrenheit) under standard atmospheric pressure conditions.
The Earth orbits the sun once every 365.25 days, which is why we have leap years every four years.
The Eiffel Tower in Paris was originally built as a temporary structure for the 1889 World's Fair.
A dozen equals twelve items, while a baker's dozen traditionally consists of thirteen items.
The Pacific Ocean is the largest and deepest of Earth's five oceanic divisions, covering about 46% of the water surface.
Regular exercise, adequate sleep, and a balanced diet are fundamental components of maintaining good health.
The human body consists of approximately 60% water, which is essential for nearly all bodily functions.
The Great Wall of China stretches over 13,000 miles and was built over many centuries by different dynasties.
Democracy is a form of government where citizens exercise power by voting to elect representatives.
The speed of sound in air at sea level is approximately 343 meters per second or 767 miles per hour.
Mount Everest is the Earth's highest mountain above sea level, standing at 8,849 meters (29,032 feet).
A year on Earth consists of four seasons: spring, summer, autumn (fall), and winter, caused by axial tilt.
The human heart pumps blood throughout the body, beating approximately 100,000 times per day on average.
The Nile River in Africa is traditionally considered the longest river in the world at about 6,650 kilometers.
Lightning is a natural electrical discharge caused by imbalances between storm clouds and the ground.
The primary colors of light are red, green, and blue, while primary colors in painting are red, yellow, and blue.
Tokyo is the capital city of Japan and one of the world's most populous metropolitan areas.
Gravity is the force that attracts objects with mass toward each other, keeping planets in orbit around stars.
The Mediterranean Sea is bordered by Europe to the north, Africa to the south, and Asia to the east.
Diamonds are formed deep underground under extreme pressure and temperature over millions of years.
The atom is the basic unit of matter, consisting of protons, neutrons, and electrons.
The Sahara Desert in North Africa is the largest hot desert in the world, covering about 9 million square kilometers.
Vaccines work by training the immune system to recognize and fight specific diseases without causing illness.
The Antarctic ice sheet contains about 90% of the world's fresh water held in ice form.
Plants produce oxygen through photosynthesis, using sunlight to convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose.
The Roman Empire, at its height, controlled much of Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.
A rainbow forms when sunlight is refracted, reflected, and dispersed in water droplets, creating a spectrum of colors.
The International Date Line is an imaginary line in the Pacific Ocean where the date changes by one day.
Earthquakes occur when tectonic plates suddenly release accumulated stress along fault lines in the Earth's crust.
The moon's gravitational pull on Earth's oceans causes tides to rise and fall approximately twice daily.
